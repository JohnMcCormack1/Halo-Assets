At a glance

Across all firms, June saw 3,428 tickets raised and 3,227 closed. The report covers all Lawfront Group (LFGR) firms plus MSP-managed Nelsons (Quiss) and Trethowans (AMS), on the service desk’s Halo dashboard scope: the five core IT teams, tickets logged in-month, with spam and no-action notifications excluded. FSP goes live on Halo this month (currently zero tickets in).

Doing well: priority-ticket SLA (P1–P3) for the LFGR in-house desk is strong at 83.1%, the ageing backlog is low at 38 tickets open beyond 30 days, and the desk closed almost as many tickets as it logged.

To improve: first-time fix for the IT Services team, from the desk’s own SQL reporting, was 63.2% in June across 1,852 resolved tickets — below the 85% target and the clearest focus area this month. SLA excludes routine P4 tickets while agents are upskilled in triage (P4 would read ~97%); first-time fix already includes all priorities.
