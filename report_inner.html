At a glance

Across all firms, June saw 3,428 tickets raised and 3,227 closed. The report covers all Lawfront Group (LFGR) firms plus MSP-managed Nelsons (Quiss) and Trethowans (AMS), on the service desk’s Halo dashboard scope: the five core IT teams, tickets logged in-month, with spam and no-action notifications excluded. The chart below breaks this down by firm; per-firm detail follows. FSP goes live on Halo this month (currently zero tickets in).

Doing well: for the LFGR in-house IT desk, priority SLA (P1–P3) is strong at 83.1%, and the desk closed almost as many tickets as it logged (2,125 against 2,181), keeping the queue under control.

To improve: first-time fix for the IT Services team, from the desk’s own SQL reporting, was 63.2% in June across 1,852 resolved tickets — below the 85% target. Ageing is the other watch item: across the reporting hubs about 426 tickets are open beyond 30 days (LFGR 38; Trethowans 388, mostly long-running 2024–25 items; Nelsons pending its all-open feed), and need clearing down. SLA excludes routine P4 while agents are upskilled in triage (P4 would read ~97%); first-time fix already includes all priorities.
