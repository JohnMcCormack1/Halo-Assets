At a glance

Across all firms, June saw 2,937 tickets raised and 2,805 closed. The report covers all Lawfront Group (LFGR) firms plus MSP-managed Nelsons (Quiss) and Trethowans (AMS). The chart below breaks this down by firm; per-firm detail follows. FSP goes live on Halo this month (currently zero tickets in).

Doing well: for the LFGR in-house IT desk (not the MSP-managed firms), first-contact resolution reached 87.3% — above the 85% target. In-house priority SLA (P1–P3) rose to 86.6% from a 72.1% baseline, its strongest month this year.

To improve: 156 tickets have been open over 30 days and need clearing down. SLA excludes routine P4 tickets while agents are upskilled in triage (P4 will become reportable in the SLA metric in time). This affects SLA only — first-time fix already includes all ticket priorities.
